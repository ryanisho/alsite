Introducing Altum Labs

By Benjamin Liker

We live in a fragile world... one with people who make mistakes, who are vulnerable, who don't always know what to do. Part of that last one is knowing who (or what) to trust- over the past decade, an exponential increase in the availability of information via entering the Social Media Era has left people with an excess of details and without any means to evaluate the validity of them. Digital natives - those who "grew up" on smartphones, social media, and other Web 2.0 technologies - have an easier time than most navigating this new world, but everyone else, and even them, still find it difficult to parse meaningful information from the glut.

Fueled by the media and governments, combined with frequent attacks on democratic systems, privacy, free speech, and societal norms (and even the perception of some of these as attacks is debated), we have seen a rapid decline in trust in the world. The Edelman Trust Barometer shows that distrust is now society's default emotion. The failure of effective governance (government) and communication (media) to unite and guide society in a trustworthy (and generally good-faith) manner has led the private sector to become the sole remaining bastion of trust. As such, businesses have a unique responsibility to break the cycles of distrust that currently run rampant through our society.

And that brings us to Altum Labs' Day 1, bottom-line mission. Our purpose - our existence - is dedicated to repairing broken bonds of society (such as trust) through innovation, transparency, kindness, respect, and good governance. I hope one day our story will be a template for how to sweepingly change the world for the better, but right now our goal is just to build a little bit of trust in each other, one scan at a time.

I'm excited for what's to come, hopefully you are too.

Benjamin
